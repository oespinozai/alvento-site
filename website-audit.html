01 — What we audit

Six areas, executive summary

The audit covers the dimensions that matter to a board, a regulator, and a member — not a generic PageSpeed score.

01

Accessibility

WCAG 2.2 AA coverage. PSBAR exposure for public-sector bodies. Landmarks, headings, ARIA, keyboard paths, touch-target sizing.

02

SEO hygiene

Titles, meta descriptions, canonical tags, Open Graph, heading hierarchy, duplicate content, indexability.

03

Performance

Core Web Vitals, mobile payload, image sizing and format, cumulative layout shift, render-blocking assets.

04

Security posture

HTTP security headers (CSP, Referrer-Policy, HSTS), mixed-content risks, rel="noopener" on external links.

05

Structured data

schema.org Organization, Event markup for congresses, Article for publications, breadcrumbs — for search and AI surfaces.

06

Content & UX

Duplicate H1s, broken images, typos, confusing CTAs, mobile navigation, long-page navigation patterns.

05 — Questions

FAQ

What does “free” actually mean?

The audit itself is free. If you want us to implement the fixes, we offer three fixed-scope remediation packages, priced in the deliverable. You are under no obligation to engage us for remediation.

How long does the audit take?

Typically 3–5 working days from confirmation of eligibility to delivery of the executive summary.

Who does the audit?

Oscar Espinoza, founder of Alvento Ltd. Audits are delivered personally — no outsourcing, no junior staff.

Is Alvento affiliated with any professional body?

No. Alvento is an independent consultancy. Oscar's domain experience in UK orthopaedics comes from three years at the National Joint Registry; audits are delivered in a personal capacity, not on behalf of any employer.

What about local businesses and small companies?

Free audits are scoped to regulated organisations. If you run a small business, the best starting point is the blog.
